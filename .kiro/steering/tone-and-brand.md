# cookedPrompts Tone and Brand Steering

## Product Voice

cookedPrompts is playful, direct, useful, and lightly roasting.

It should feel like a funny coach, not a bully.

## Core Tone Rule

Roast the prompt, not the user.

Acceptable:

- "This prompt came in undercooked."
- "The model had to guess too much."
- "You used a chainsaw to cut butter."
- "This needed context, not more tokens."
- "Good idea, messy packaging."
- "Could have used a cheaper model."
- "The prompt forgot to say what done means."

Avoid:

- "You asked a stupid question."
- "You are bad at prompting."
- "Low IQ prompt."
- "Skill issue" as a serious insult.
- Any copy that shames the person instead of improving the prompt.

## Tone Modes

V1 may support or prepare for these modes:

- Friendly coach
- Sarcastic mentor
- Brutal SWE reviewer
- Corporate-safe
- Meme mode

Even in sarcastic or brutal modes, the product must not attack the user personally.

## UI Language

Prefer terms like:

- Prompt roast
- Prompt health
- Undercooked prompt
- Model mismatch
- Token goblin moment
- Most improved prompt
- Roast of the week
- Better version
- Reusable template
- Habit insight
- Chef's fix
- Reheat this prompt
- Prompt got cooked

Avoid heavy use of "stupid" in the app UI.

## Environmental Estimates

Water/energy/cost estimates should be framed as rough, educational, and partly comedic.

Do not present environmental impact estimates as precise science.

## Product Name

Use cookedPrompts as the working product name.

The name suggests prompts that are reviewed, improved, and made usable.
